    Alex Gimenez Romero

    Hi there! I am Alex Giménez Romero, a physicist trained at the Autonomous University of Barcelona from 2015 to 2019. I had the pleasure to get absorbed into the somewhat magic world of statistical physics from the hands of Vicens Méndez, Juan Camacho and Jordi Faraudo, which led me to discover the even more fascinating world of Complex Systems at IFISC, where I did my master’s from 2019 to 2020.

    Now I’m pursuing a PhD in Physics of Complex Systems at IFISC under the supervision of Dr. Manuel Matías.

    My research

    My current research focuses on developing mathematical and computational models in the context of epidemiology and ecology. In particular, I am modelling the epidemics causing the Mass Mortality Event of Pinna nobilis, and the emergent epidemics in almond trees and vineyards caused by the bacterium Xylella fastidiosa in Europe. Besides, I am developing Machine Learning models to detect and classify different seagrass and algae species from satellite images and analyzing worldwide spatial data of coral reefs. Check the post about this project for further details!

    This research falls inside the CYCLE and SEDIMENT projects hosted at IFISC.
